2024 DSE Paper I Q18

Question Summary: 3D folding problem involving a quadrilateral $PQRS$ folded along $QS$.

$PQ=12, PS=10, QR=13, \angle QPS=82^\circ, \angle QRS=65^\circ$.

(a) Find $QS$ and $\angle RQS$.

(b) Folded along $QS$ such that angle between planes $PQS$ and $QRS$ is $80^\circ$. Find distance from $R$ to plane $PQS$ and check if $PX > 8$.

Part (a)(i) - Length of $QS$

In $\triangle PQS$, by Cosine Rule:

$QS^2 = 12^2 + 10^2 - 2(12)(10)\cos(82^\circ)$

$QS \approx 14.5126... \approx 14.5 \text{ cm}$

Part (a)(ii) - $\angle RQS$

In $\triangle RQS$, by Sine Rule:

$\frac{13}{\sin\angle RSQ} = \frac{14.5126}{\sin 65^\circ}$

After finding $\angle RSQ$, then $\angle RQS = 180^\circ - 65^\circ - \angle RSQ$.

$\angle RQS \approx 60.8^\circ$

Part (b)(i) - Distance from $R$ to plane $PQS$

Let $h$ be the perpendicular height from $R$ to $QS$.

$h = 13 \sin(60.849^\circ) \approx 11.353 \text{ cm}$

Shortest distance to plane = $h \sin(80^\circ)$

Distance $\approx 11.18 \text{ cm}$

Part (b)(ii) - Distance $PX > 8$ cm?

Find the shortest distance from $P$ to the line $QS$. Compare this with the relative position of $X$.

Calculation shows the minimum distance exceeds 8 cm. The claim is **Correct**.
