2024 DSE Paper I Q18

Part (a) PQRS a quadrilateral, where $PQ = 12\text{ cm}$, $PS = 10\text{ cm}$, $QR = 13\text{ cm}$, $\angle QPS = 82^\circ$ and $\angle QRS = 65^\circ$.
Note: You are viewing the 2D layout.
(a)(i) Find the length of $QS$.

Using Cosine Rule in $\triangle PQS$:
$QS^2 = {PQ^2 + PS^2 - 2(PQ)(PS)\cos(\angle QPS)}$

$QS^2 = {12^2 + 10^2 - 2(12)(10)\cos(82^\circ)}$

$QS = \sqrt{{12^2 + 10^2 - 2(12)(10)\cos(82^\circ)}}\text{ cm} \approx 14.513 \text{ cm} $

$QS \approx 14.5 \text{ cm}$

(a)(ii) Find $\angle RQS$.

Using Sine Rule in $\triangle QRS$:
$$ \frac{QR}{\sin(\angle QSR)} = \frac{QS}{\sin(\angle QRS)} \implies \frac{13}{\sin(\angle QSR)} = \frac{14.513}{\sin(65^\circ)}$$

$\sin \angle QSR \approx 0.811878935$

$\angle QSR \approx 54.280^\circ\text{ or }\angle QSR \approx 125.72^\circ\text{ (rejected)}$

$\angle RQS = 180^\circ - \angle QRS - \angle QSR \approx 180^\circ - 65^\circ - 54.280^\circ$

$\angle RQS \approx 60.7^\circ$

Part (b) The metal sheet $PQRS$ is now folded along $QS$. It is given that the angle between plane $PQS$ and plane $QRS$ is $80^\circ$.
Note: You are viewing the 2D layout.
(b)(i) Find the shortest distance from $R$ to the plane $PQS$.

Consider $\triangle QRS$.
Let $T$ be a point on $QS$ such that $RT \perp QS$.

In right-angled $\triangle RTQ$,
$RT = QR\sin\angle RQS \approx 13\sin\angle 54.280^\circ \approx 13.340\text{ cm}$

Let the shortest distance be $h$
$h = RT\sin(80^\circ) \approx 11.168 \text{ cm}$

$h \approx 11.2 \text{ cm}$

(b)(i) Claim: distance between $P$ and $X$ exceeds $8\text{ cm}$. Is it correct?

$PX_{\text{min}} = 12 \sin(\angle PQS) \sin(80^\circ) \approx 8.0643 \text{ cm}$

$PR \approx 15.2 \text{ cm}$

The claim is **Correct**.
