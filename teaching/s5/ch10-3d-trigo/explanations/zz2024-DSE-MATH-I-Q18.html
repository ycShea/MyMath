Question Summary (UpdateForNewExercise) Delete this before released.

PQRS is a thin quadrilateral metal sheet, where PQ = 12 cm , PS = 10 cm , QR = 13 cm , QPS = 82° and QRS = 65° .

Full Question (UpdateForNewExercise) Delete this before released.

Solution 1/4 (UpdateForNewExercise) Delete this before released.

(a)(i) Find the length of QS.

In $\triangle PQS$, by the Cosine Rule:

$QS^2 = 12^2 + 10^2 - 2(12)(10)\cos(82^\circ)$

$QS \approx 14.5126 \text{ cm}$

Solution 2/4 (UpdateForNewExercise) Delete this before released.

(a)(ii) Find ∠RQS.

In $\triangle RQS$, by the Sine Rule:

$\frac{13}{\sin(\angle RSQ)} = \frac{14.5126}{\sin(65^\circ)}$

$\angle RSQ \approx 54.2687^\circ$

$\angle RQS = 180^\circ - 65^\circ - 54.2687^\circ \approx 60.7^\circ$

Solution 3/4 (UpdateForNewExercise) Delete this before released.

(b)(i)Find the shortest distance from R to the plane PQS .

Let $h$ be the perpendicular height from $R$ to $QS$:

$h = 13 \sin(60.7^\circ) \approx 11.3406 \text{ cm}$

Shortest distance to plane = $h \sin(80^\circ)$

Distance $\approx 11.2 \text{ cm}$

(b)(ii) Let X be any point lying on the plane QRS . Someone claims that the distance between P and X exceeds 8 cm . Is the claim correct? Explain your answer.

Calculate minimum distance from $P$ to plane $QRS$:

$PX = 12 \sin(\angle PQS) \sin(80^\circ) \approx 8.0643 \text{ cm}$

Since $8.06 > 8$, the claim is **Correct**.
