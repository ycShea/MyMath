2024 DSE Paper I Q18

Question Summary 1/2 (UpdateForNewExercise) Delete this before released.

PQRS a quadrilateral, where $PQ = 12\text{ cm}$, $PS = 10\text{ cm}$, $QR = 13\text{ cm}$, $\angle QPS = 82^\circ$ and $\angle QRS = 65^\circ$.

Solution 1/4 (UpdateForNewExercise) Delete this before released.

(a)(i) Find the length of $QS$.

$QS = \sqrt{12^2 + 10^2 - 2(12)(10)\cos(82^\circ)} \approx 14.5126 \text{ cm}$

Solution 2/4 (UpdateForNewExercise) Delete this before released.

(a)(ii) Find $\angle RQS$.

$\frac{13}{\sin(\angle RSQ)} = \frac{14.5126}{\sin(65^\circ)} \implies \angle RQS \approx 60.7^\circ$

Question Summary 2/2 (UpdateForNewExercise) Delete this before released.

The metal sheet $PQRS$ is now folded along $QS$. It is given that the angle between plane $PQS$ and plane $QRS$ is $80^\circ$.

Solution 3/4 (UpdateForNewExercise) Delete this before released.

(b)(i) Find the shortest distance from $R$ to the plane $PQS$.

Let $h$ be the perpendicular height from $R$ to $QS$:

$h = 13 \sin(60.7^\circ) \approx 11.3406 \text{ cm}$

Shortest distance = $h \sin(80^\circ) \approx 11.2 \text{ cm}$

Solution 4/4 (UpdateForNewExercise) Delete this before released.

(b)(ii) Claim: distance between $P$ and $X$ exceeds $8\text{ cm}$. Is it correct?

$PX_{\text{min}} = 12 \sin(\angle PQS) \sin(80^\circ) \approx 8.0643 \text{ cm}$

Since $8.06 > 8$, the claim is **Correct**.
