Instruction: In the figure, ABCDEF is a right triangular prism. P is a point lying on AE. If AB=AC=12, AP=9, EP=5 and BD=2k, find the area of $\Delta BDP$.

Step 1: Find AE (Total Height)
$AE = AP + EP = 9 + 5 = 14$
Note: For a right prism, $AE = BF = CD = 14$.

Step 2: Find DP (Pythagoras in $\Delta DEP$)
$DP = \sqrt{DE^2 + EP^2} = \sqrt{AC^2 + EP^2}$
$DP = \sqrt{12^2 + 5^2} = \sqrt{144 + 25} = \sqrt{169} = \mathbf{13}$

Step 3: Find BP (Pythagoras in $\Delta BAP$)
$BP = \sqrt{AB^2 + AP^2} = \sqrt{12^2 + 9^2}$
$BP = \sqrt{144 + 81} = \sqrt{225} = \mathbf{15}$

Sides of $\Delta BDP$ are now: $BD=2k$, $DP=13$, $BP=15$.
